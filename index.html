Why Carb Counting Matters

For people with type 1 diabetes, carbs affect blood sugar a lot. They're basically sugar fuel If you know how much you're eating, it's easier to take the right insulin and avoid sugar highs or crashes SnackTrack helps you figure that out easily

Reminders

Snack on time without overthinking

Carbs turn into sugar in your blood. They give energy but can raise blood sugar fast especially important to watch if you’re managing type 1 diabetes

Simple carbs (like juice or sweets) hit your blood sugar fast
Complex carbs (like oats or beans) take longer and keep you full

Carbs can sneak into stuff like:

  • Sauces
  • Fruit juice
  • Yoghurts or bars

Always check labels — small things add up

  • Look for nutrition info online or ask
  • Stick to meals you know the carb count for
  • Ask to swap chips for salad, it’s fine

If you're not sure, it’s okay, just use stuff like:

  • Carbs & Cals app
  • Food packaging
  • This guide

It gets easier with practice

why carb counting matters

Matching insulin to carbs makes blood sugar easier to manage, especially with basal/bolus insulin

How it works

Count carbs by grams or in 10g chunks, ask your diabetes team what’s best for you

Insulin to carb ratio

If your ratio is 1:10, and your meal is 70g of carbs, then you need 7 units of insulin

3 Ways to Start

  • Figure out which foods have carbs
  • Read food labels
  • Practice guessing with food lists

Final tips

  • Check food labels often
  • Use apps or a kitchen scale
  • Keep learning and ask questions
